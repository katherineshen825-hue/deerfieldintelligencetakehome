## Cross Functional Coordination Model

Overview

- Deerfield Intelligence operates like a high-velocity startup inside a private equity firm. The Director of Operations will serve as the primary interface for both pod leads (Priya and Marcus) and the support functions (James, Elena, and Sarah).

### Elena Rodriguez, HR

**Ops owns:** Recruiting pipeline coordination, DI-specific onboarding execution, performance review scheduling, team health monitoring, and translating David's headcount vision into an executable hiring roadmap.

**Elena owns:** Firm-wide HR policy, compensation and leveling frameworks, legal risk assessment, and benefits and payroll.

**Boundaries:** Ops never makes unilateral decisions on compensation or titles outside Elena's framework. Ops makes the Deerfield HR framework move faster for DI instead of working around.

**Standing cadence:**

- Biweekly sync after initial operating cadences are established
- Monthly hiring pipeline review
- Quarterly people review covering retention risk and performance documentation status

**What good looks like:** Elena receives a complete hiring roadmap in week one, never gets a surprise requisition on a Friday afternoon, and describes the relationship as proactive rather than reactive.

### James Okonkwo, Finance

**Ops owns:** Vendor registry maintenance, Intent to Spend process, monthly pre-close sync, spending authority enforcement, and real-time budget visibility for pod leads.

**James owns:** Invoice processing, firm-wide financial reporting, budget approval above threshold, and CFO-level escalations.

**Boundaries:** Ops is a filter not a middleman. 

**Standing cadence:**

- Monthly pre-close sync: 30 minutes covering spend summary, new commitments, and upcoming renewals
- Quarterly vendor audit review

**What good looks like:** James receives the pre-close sync every month without asking, the vendor registry stays current without him maintaining it, and he can walk into a partners meeting and explain DI's spend rather than shrugging.

### Sarah Kim, Compliance

**Ops owns:** Compliance intake process, project pipeline visibility, vendor compliance documentation, model inventory maintenance, and access control coordination.

**Sarah owns:** Risk assessment and legal sign-off, regulatory interpretation, final deployment approval, and audit response.

**Boundaries:** Ops never makes compliance judgments. Ops is responsible for surfacing potential compliance issues to Sarah.

**Standing cadence:**

- Monthly compliance review covering new data sources, model deployments, and API integrations
- Ad hoc for any MNPI flag, data provenance concern, or deployment hold

**What good looks like:** Sarah receives a completed five-field intake form at the start of every project, never discovers a live product at the finish line, and describes compliance as a feature of how the team builds rather than a gate they visit at the end.

### Investment Partners and Firm Leadership

**Ops owns:** Concierge intake experience for investment partner requests, capacity translation before commitments are made, and surfacing any bypass of the intake process to David immediately.

**David owns:** The political conversation with managing partners about the new engagement model, final authority on strategic trade-offs, and escalation for partners who push back on the process.

**Boundaries:** Ops manages the process. David manages the relationships. Ops manages the intake to prioritize requests and David steps in to further push back on managing directors if necessary.

**Standing cadence:**

- Investment partner presentation at end of month one
- Quarterly review of intake adoption and pod lead satisfaction with demand management

**What good looks like:** Pod leads describe the shadow roadmap as a problem that no longer exists and Marcus can walk into any meeting with the data to back up a yes or a no.

### Priya Sharma, AI Investment Systems

**Ops owns:** Removing operational overhead from Priya's plate so she can function as a lead engineer again. This includes vendor and procurement coordination, compliance intake facilitation, cross-pod dependency management, onboarding execution for new pod hires, and documentation of the pod's institutional knowledge.

**Priya owns:** All technical and architectural decisions, model development and deployment, data pipeline design, and the quality of AI outputs informing investment decisions.

**Boundaries:** Ops does not add processes that slows down engineering velocity or increase bureaucracy. 

**Standing cadence:**

- Biweekly cross-pod sync with Priya and Marcus: 30 minutes covering shared capacity, upcoming dependencies, and anything needing ops support
- Ad hoc for any vendor request, compliance intake question, or cross-pod conflict requiring triage

**What good looks like:** Ops is a great backend where you don't necessarily see it, but everything runs smoother and faster because it's there.

### Marcus Thompson, Portfolio Enablement

**Ops owns:** Building the demand management infrastructure Marcus needs to stop absorbing unlimited external requests and start operating strategically. This includes the portfolio company intake process, scope of work templates, cross-pod capacity visibility, and preparing David for the investment partner conversation that resets how external demand flows into the team.

**Marcus owns:** Portfolio company relationships, feature scoping and delivery, external stakeholder communication, and the strategic direction of Portfolio Enablement's product work.

**Boundaries:** Ops builds the framework and the tools but Marcus owns the external relationships. Ops can redirect an investment partner to the intake process but cannot manage the nuance of a long-standing investor relationship on Marcus's behalf. The goal is to give Marcus a system he can point to when saying no.

**Standing cadence:**

- Biweekly cross-pod sync with Priya and Marcus: 30 minutes covering shared capacity, upcoming dependencies, and anything needing ops support
- Monthly portfolio company pipeline review: active engagements, scope status, and upcoming commitments that may require Priya's pod

**What good looks like:** Marcus can explicitly tell investment partners what the capacity and timeline is for various projects. He is operating with full visibility instead of a gut feel then doing damage control.
